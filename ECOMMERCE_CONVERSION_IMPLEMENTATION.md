# E-commerce conversion strategy and implementation

## Conversion goal

The primary conversion is a qualified commerce operator sending enough context for Henry to identify the first useful investigation or system.

The page should not try to sell seven systems at once. It should move a prospect through five decisions:

1. This person understands the operational pressure behind a growing store.
2. The work is tied to revenue, time, or decision quality rather than AI novelty.
3. There is credible evidence that Henry can design and deliver controlled systems.
4. The first engagement is bounded enough to feel safe.
5. Contacting Henry will produce a useful reply rather than a generic sales sequence.

## Audience and buying objections

### Primary prospect

A growing Shopify founder, operator, head of e-commerce, or support/operations lead whose store has enough volume and data to expose repeated work, missed follow-up, stock risk, slow decisions, or margin pressure.

### Questions the page must answer

| Prospect question | Signal required |
| --- | --- |
| Do you understand my kind of operation? | Specific commerce problems and fit criteria |
| What business outcome changes? | Revenue, time, and decision outcomes |
| Why should I trust you? | Built work, client delivery evidence, and honest status labels |
| Are these real results or concepts? | Explicit built, NDA-safe, modeled, and concept labels |
| Where would we begin? | Revenue Leak Audit as the bounded diagnostic entry |
| Will this force a new platform? | Existing-stack-first commitment |
| Will AI act without us? | Approval, escalation, logs, and least-privilege access |
| How much risk am I accepting? | Written scope, access, fee, timeline, and exclusions before work |
| What happens when I click? | Commerce-specific form and one-business-day direct reply |

## Verified evidence available now

The implementation uses only commerce evidence already documented in the portfolio:

- Clear Skin Concierge — built product with a public repository, typed commerce actions, and explicit confirmation.
- CartPilot — interactive guided-selling concept, clearly identified as a concept and tied to Shopify's documented catalog and cart capabilities.
- MarginGuard — modeled commerce scenario, clearly identified as a model rather than a client result.

No revenue lift, client testimonial, price, delivery duration, or brand logo was invented.

The proof section also includes a separate market-evidence row. These are research facts, not Henry's project outcomes:

- Baymard Institute's 2026 benchmark reports a 70.22% average documented cart abandonment rate across 50 studies.
- Shopify's profit-report documentation confirms that order-level profitability can involve product and shipping charges, store-paid costs, duties, discounts, and refunds.

Sources:

## Commerce-proof revision and critique

### Draft

The first commerce-only revision replaced the nonprofit operations case with CartPilot and added a primary source to every card:

- Clear Skin links to public source code.
- CartPilot links to Shopify's Storefront MCP documentation.
- MarginGuard links to Shopify's profit-report documentation.

It also added two researched commerce facts below the project cards.

### Critique

That structure created three risks:

1. A platform document can prove technical feasibility, but it cannot prove Henry delivered a measured client result.
2. An industry benchmark can prove the commercial problem is common, but it cannot prove Henry improved that benchmark.
3. The evidence mix still contains only one public built commerce product. CartPilot and MarginGuard demonstrate product and systems thinking, not independently verified outcomes.

### Final revision

The final section now uses two visibly different evidence layers:

- **Work evidence** — three commerce examples, each with an explicit built, concept, or modeled label and a link to inspect the work.
- **Market evidence** — a separate bordered row headed “Market evidence, not client outcomes,” with direct source links.

The cards use “Inspect the work” for Henry's artifact and “Verify…” for the external basis. This prevents borrowed research authority from being presented as personal delivery proof.

## First implementation draft

The first pass added:

- A four-point trust strip directly after the hero.
- A proof section separating a built commerce product, an interactive commerce concept, and a modeled commerce system.
- A productized Revenue Leak Audit section covering scope, outputs, commercial terms, and store safety.
- Four additional FAQs covering cost and timeline setting, access, customer data, and the post-contact experience.
- Stronger hero and final CTA promises.
- A commerce-specific contact modal with tailored heading, field language, button copy, response promise, and success state.

## Critique of the first draft

### What worked

- The page no longer asks the prospect to trust attractive concepts alone.
- The strongest proof is visible before the offer gallery.
- The Revenue Leak Audit gives an uncertain buyer a smaller first decision.
- The form now continues the commerce conversation instead of switching to generic portfolio language.
- The copy avoids unsupported commercial claims.

### What was still weak

1. The “See the evidence” link landed at the proof animation trigger instead of the proof heading. The prospect saw the end of the previous section and only the proof eyebrow.
2. The portfolio assistant remained visible over the contact modal and covered part of the submit area.
3. The new commercial language could still be mistaken for a fixed package if it implied a price or timeline that has not been defined.
4. The page became longer, so every added section had to earn its place.

## Final revision

The second pass:

- Moved the proof animation trigger inside the section so section navigation lands with the proof headline and explanation visible.
- Hides the portfolio assistant while the contact modal is open.
- Keeps the Revenue Leak Audit commercially bounded without inventing a price or delivery promise.
- Keeps all eight FAQs collapsed except the first, preserving scanability.
- Retains only sections that answer a distinct buyer question: outcome, evidence, available systems, bounded entry, process, fit, objections, and action.

## Final page argument

### 1. Hero — relevance and value

**Headline:** Your store should grow without taking more of your week.

**Primary CTA:** Find my first opportunity.

**Secondary CTA:** See the evidence.

**Risk note:** Direct reply from Henry within one business day. No forced platform migration.

### 2. Trust strip — operating principles

- Shopify is the strongest fit.
- The current stack comes first.
- People approve risky actions.
- Built, modeled, and concept work stays labeled.

### 3. Outcomes — business value

- Recover revenue.
- Reclaim the week.
- Operate with clarity.

### 4. Evidence — belief

**Section promise:** Three ways to inspect the thinking.

Each card shows its real evidence status and links to the complete case:

- Built commerce product / public repository.
- Interactive conversational-commerce concept.
- Modeled commerce-intelligence scenario.

Each card also links to either public source code or the primary platform documentation that supports its technical or commercial basis. A separate market-evidence row labels research as research so industry statistics cannot be mistaken for client results.

### 5. Systems — solution range

The seven systems remain available, but the copy makes clear that the prospect should automate the expensive pressure point rather than automate everywhere.

### 6. Revenue Leak Audit — bounded first engagement

The page now explains:

- Scope.
- Outputs.
- How commercial terms are set.
- How live-store risk is controlled.

The CTA asks for an audit scope rather than asking the prospect to buy an undefined service.

### 7. Process — delivery confidence

Diagnose, build, and prove remain the delivery model. The result—not the novelty—is the measurement point.

### 8. Fit — qualification

The page identifies the operating patterns that justify automation. This helps unsuitable prospects opt out and gives suitable prospects language for their problem.

### 9. FAQ — objection handling

The final FAQ set covers:

- Existing applications.
- Commerce platform fit.
- Starting point.
- Human control.
- Timeline and cost setting.
- Access.
- Customer data.
- What happens after contact.

### 10. Final CTA — response certainty

**Headline:** Find the first commerce problem worth fixing.

**CTA:** Show me where to start.

**Response promise:** A direct reply within one business day with the first evidence to inspect and the most practical next step.

## Contact handoff

Commerce CTAs now open a tailored modal:

- “Find your first commerce opportunity.”
- “Where is the store under pressure?”
- “Send commerce brief.”
- Direct-reply and one-business-day expectation.
- The originating commerce context remains attached to the submission.

Generic portfolio and employment enquiries keep the generic project variant.

## Facts still needed before stronger commercial claims can be published

These should remain absent until Henry supplies verified values:

- Starting price or investment range.
- Typical audit duration.
- Typical build duration.
- Named commerce clients or approved logos.
- Approved testimonials.
- Measured revenue, margin, conversion, support-time, or inventory results.
- Ongoing support options and service levels.

Once available, the strongest next conversion improvement is a compact “What clients achieved” module between the trust strip and outcomes. It should use real baselines, measured results, dates, and scope notes.

## Measurement plan

The next analytics pass should track:

- Hero CTA opened.
- Evidence section visited.
- Evidence case opened.
- Commerce system opened.
- Revenue Leak Audit scope requested.
- FAQ opened by question.
- Commerce brief started.
- Commerce brief submitted successfully.

The key funnel is:

`commerce landing → evidence or audit detail → commerce modal → successful brief`

The page should be judged on qualified enquiry rate, not raw CTA clicks.
